Lagos is known for its diverse cultural landscape shaped by the Yoruba people and a mixture of ethnic groups from across Nigeria. The city is a hub for music, fashion and film, contributing significantly to the growth of Afrobeats and Nollywood. Festivals such as the Eyo Festival celebrate traditional heritage with elaborate costumes and processions. Food culture in Lagos includes dishes like jollof rice, amala, efo riro, asun and street foods such as suya, akara and puff-puff. Social life in Lagos is lively, with nightlife centers in Victoria Island, Lekki and Ikeja.
